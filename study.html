Books

I've been keeping track of the books that I've read since 2016 (I've added in some books previous to that as well). Here's my library!

Book Title Author Genre Date read Comments
100 Things Every Designer Should Know About People Susan Weinschenk Non-Fiction 2017 Recommended to me by my sister if I wanted to learn the basics of user experience, this book gave me a glimpse of what it takes to be a designer.
Grit Angela Duckworth Psychology/Self-help 2017 One of my favorite books to date. Grit is a paragon of perseverence and passion.
Don't Make Me Think Steve Krugg Non-Fiction 2017 Another must read design book. I took lots of notes!
Elon Musk Ashlee Vance Biography 2016 Elon Musk is one of my heroes so much that I vow to buy a Tesla in the future. I loved getting a glimpse of his personal life and how it connected to his career.
C.S. Lewis: A Life Alister E. McGrath Biography 2016 C.S. Lewis is also one of my role models, but he always had such a mystical quality about him. I loved reading about his personal life (and quirks) in this book. It also got me started on rereading the Narnia series.
How Google Works Eric Scmidt Non-Fiction 2016 The title got me interested. I really wanted to know how google worked.
The Power of Habit Charles Duhigg Psychology/Self-Help 2016 I started reading lots of self-help books during this time to improve my productivity. This was an amazing one that really helped me set my daily schedules.
Mastery Robert Greene Psychology/Self-Help 2016 An amazing book for sticking to your craft. I believe it's the origin of the 10,000 hour rule.
Smarter, Faster, Better Charles Duhigg Psychology/Self-Help 2016 Another book that I started reading because of his other book.
The Innovators Walter Isaacson Psychology/Self-Help 2016 I wanted to learn more about the founders of computers and the internet. It was amazing to learn about that history.
Tuesdays with Morrie Mitch Albom Inspirational 2016
The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy Author Douglas Adams 2016 I read this book because Elon Musk rated it as one of the best books he's ever read. It's also quoted quite frequently in reddit comments.
When Breath Becomes Air Paul Kalanithi Autobiography 2016 I understand why a doctor's journey is so difficult. Also convinced me that I shouldn't ever be one and gave me far more respect for those who become one.
A House in the Sky Amanda Lindhout Autobiography 2016 Very graphic picture on what it's like to be a hostage. I definitely started to look at feminism from a different point of view after reading this book.
A Faithful Father Dwyane Wade Autobiography 2016 As one of my favorite basketball players, Dwyane Wade went into depth about his struggles in Chicago. It taught me the importance of a father role model for children.
An Abundance of Katherines John Green Fiction 2016
Bucking the Sarge Christopher Paul Curtis Fiction 2016
The Hunger Games Suzanne Collins Fiction 2016 Reread (from 2010?)
Catching Fire Suzanne Collins Fiction 2016 Reread
Mockingjay Suzanne Collins Fiction 2016 Reread
The Lion, the Witch, the Wardrobe C.S. Lewis Fiction 2016 I've actually never finished this book or the following, so I resolved to finish all of them.
Prince Caspian C.S. Lewis Fiction 2016
Voyage of the Dawn Treader C.S. Lewis Fiction 2016
The Silver Chair C.S. Lewis Fiction 2016
Unstoppable Bill Nye Non-Fiction 2016 A great book about climate change. I heard about it from the author while he was on one of my favorite podcasts, Startalk Radio.
A Boy and His Horse C.S. Lewis Fiction 2016
The Giver Lois Lowry Fiction 2016 I just felt like reading a classic.
The Magician's Nephew C.S. Lewis Fiction 2016
The Last Battle C.S. Lewis Fiction 2016
Failure is not an Option Gene Kranz Autobiography 2016 After reading an article about SpaceX, I started wondering about the history of space travel...
The Wind-up Bird Chronicles Haruki Murakami Fiction 2015 Recommended by my very eccentric friend. This book gave me a taste of Japanese fiction.
Bejamin Franklin: An American Life Walter Isaacson Biography 2014
Einstein Walter Isaacson Biography 2014
What the Dog Saw Malcolm Gladwell Psychology/Self-Help 2013
Outliers Malcolm Gladwell Psychology/Self-Help 2013 This is the book that really got me into Malcolm Gladwell.
David and Goliath Malcolm Gladwell Psychology/Self-Help 2013
The Tipping Point Malcolm Gladwell Psychology/Self-Help 2013
Blink Malcolm Gladwell Psychology/Self-Help 2013
Steve Jobs Walter Isaacson Biography 2013 I loved reading about Steve Job's personal life. I was fascinated with his journey from an orphaned misfit to creative mastermind.
The Omnivore's Dilemma Michael Pollan Psychology/Self-Help 2013
A Short History of Nearly Everything Bill Bryson Non-Fiction 2013 The most comprehensive picture of the universe that I've ever read.
